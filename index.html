Teaching Assistance Through Socialy Assistive Robotics (TSAR) for ASD

1 in 160 children may have an Autism Spectrum Disorder, aka ASD (1) , and present special educational resources are insufficient

Socially Assistive Robotics (SARs)(2) is an emerging field of robotics that focusses on providing assistance to users through social interaction, like a human assistant.

The motivation to introduce technology in autism intervention programs is the affinity that ASD children have towards structured and predictable environments and the structure and consistency that is intrinsic in robots. Moreover, with interventions designed with social robots, human-intensive and repetitive therapies can be automated. This can be a boon in resource-challenged countries such as India.

Socially Assistive Robotics have been used for ASD Interventions, but such robots can be expensive.

We thus aim to use low-cost commercially available robots as Social Robots in autism education.
